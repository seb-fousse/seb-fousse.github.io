Stereoscopic Images

Photos Taken With Multiple Lenses

Stereoscopic images are images that are percieved as 3D, despite being made of 2 dimensional images. This is thanks to binocular vision and how the brain interprets 2D visual information as a 3D representation. Think of those 1800's binoculars with two photos inside. Lenticular printing was later developed (i.e. those bookmarks you find at gift shops, where the image moves if you wiggle it). In the 80's, cameras with multiple lenses were developed to take photos that could be turned into lenticular prints, with the Nimslo being one of the most popular. Someone later figured out that you could create gifs using the images taken with these cameras. The images above are a handful of 'wigglegrams' that I've taken.
