Self-Developed Film

Some Darkroom Action

In highschool I became friendly with the photography teacher. As I seemed keen and the darkroom at school was going mostly unused, he made sure it was always unlocked for me. He taught me how to load 35mm film from a reel into a canister, how to develop B&W film, how to use a film enlarger to create prints on photosensitive paper, and how to make tools to dodge and burn areas of an image. I later figured out how to use the scanners in the art room, in combination with a film holder, to scan the developed film. The photos above are a selection from a trip I took with my friends to Milan and Florence. I used everything my photography teacher taught me to prep and produce these photos. If you'd like a full resolution copy of a photo for any reason, feel free to reach out!
